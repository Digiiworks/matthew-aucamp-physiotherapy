The Physiotherapist

Evidence-Based Care, Built on Five Years of Clinical Practice

I qualified with a Bachelor of Science in Physiotherapy from the University of the Free State in 2019, graduating as a member of the Golden Key International Honour Society. From the very beginning, I knew I wanted to work in musculoskeletal physiotherapy — the kind of work where you genuinely change someone's quality of life by getting them moving again.

After completing my community service year at Livingstone Tertiary Hospital here in Port Elizabeth, I spent the next five years working across some of Gqeberha's most respected private physiotherapy practices — Deidre Steyn Physiotherapists, Caroline Swart Physiotherapy, and most recently The Back Clinic, where I deepened my specialisation in spinal rehabilitation and earned my dual DBC certifications.

In January 2026 I opened my own practice at 64 Main Road, Walmer. My approach is simple: I listen carefully, I assess thoroughly, and I treat the cause — not just the symptom. Every patient gets the time, attention, and individualised programme they need to recover properly and stay recovered.

DBC stands for Dynamic Back Care — a Finnish-developed, internationally recognised spinal rehabilitation system that combines clinical assessment, progressive resistance training, and patient education into a structured evidence-based protocol. It is used by leading physiotherapy practices in over 30 countries and is one of the most rigorously researched approaches to chronic spinal pain available today.

Becoming DBC Certified involves an intensive postgraduate training programme, supervised clinical hours, and a formal competency assessment. The dual certification — for both cervical (neck) and lumbar (back) protocols — is held by only a small number of practitioners across South Africa, and even fewer in the Eastern Cape.

For patients, this means you are receiving care grounded in current scientific evidence, delivered by a practitioner who has demonstrated advanced clinical competency in exactly the conditions you're dealing with. It is not a quick fix, and it is not a fad — it is the standard of care that spinal physiotherapy should aspire to.

Philosophy

My Approach to Physiotherapy

Evidence-Based Practice

Every treatment decision is grounded in current research. Physiotherapy is a rapidly evolving science, and staying up to date with the literature is non-negotiable. If a technique no longer has clinical support, I don't use it — regardless of how popular it might be.

That commitment to evidence is also why I pursued DBC certification: it remains one of the most rigorously researched spinal rehabilitation protocols in the world.

Manual Therapy First

Hands-on assessment and treatment is the foundation of musculoskeletal physiotherapy. Joint mobilisation, soft-tissue work and myofascial release deliver immediate improvements in movement and pain — and they tell me things about your body that no machine can.

Manual therapy is then paired with targeted exercise to make those gains last beyond the session.

Patient Education

You should leave every appointment understanding what's happening in your body, what we're doing about it, and what you can do at home to accelerate recovery. Patients who understand their condition consistently get better outcomes.

My job isn't just to treat you — it's to give you the knowledge and tools to manage your own body for the long term.
