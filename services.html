Musculoskeletal Assessment & Treatment in Gqeberha

A thorough musculoskeletal assessment is the foundation of effective physiotherapy. Most pain and dysfunction is mechanical — meaning it comes from how your body moves, loads and holds itself, not from a single isolated injury. Identifying the real driver of your symptoms is the difference between temporary relief and lasting recovery.

At your first appointment we'll go through a detailed history, assess posture, joint range, muscle length, strength and movement patterns, and run any specific orthopaedic tests required to confirm a working diagnosis. From there I'll explain what I've found and walk you through a treatment plan you'll actually understand.

Treatment combines manual therapy, individualised exercise prescription, and patient education — all delivered in a relaxed, unhurried clinical environment in the heart of Walmer.

Who This Helps

  • Persistent or recurring pain you can't explain
  • Joint stiffness limiting daily activities
  • Postural and movement-related dysfunction
  • Muscle imbalances and overuse injuries
  • General "I just don't feel right" presentations

What to Expect

  • 60-minute initial assessment
  • Detailed history and physical examination
  • Working diagnosis explained in plain language
  • Treatment plan tailored to your goals
  • Same-session manual therapy where appropriate

Manual Therapy

Manual therapy is the hands-on side of physiotherapy — and it remains one of the most effective tools we have for restoring movement and reducing pain. Joint mobilisations, soft-tissue release, myofascial work and trigger-point techniques can produce immediate, measurable improvements.

Used on its own, manual therapy is short-lived. Used in combination with targeted exercise and patient education, it forms the backbone of evidence-based physiotherapy. That's exactly how I use it.

Every manual therapy technique I use has clinical evidence behind it. If a technique is no longer supported by the literature, I don't use it — regardless of how popular it might be.

Who This Helps

  • Acute joint stiffness or restricted range of motion
  • Chronic muscle tension and trigger points
  • Spinal pain and postural dysfunction
  • Soft-tissue tightness following injury
  • Headaches with cervical (neck) origin

What to Expect

  • Hands-on assessment to identify dysfunction
  • Joint mobilisation and soft-tissue work
  • Immediate post-treatment re-assessment
  • Home exercises to maintain the gains
  • Clear explanation of what was done and why

Injury Rehabilitation in Port Elizabeth

Returning from injury isn't just about waiting for pain to settle. It's a structured progression through clearly defined stages — and skipping any of them is the most common reason people re-injure themselves.

I work with patients across the full spectrum of musculoskeletal injury, from acute soft-tissue tears to chronic overuse conditions. Each rehabilitation programme is built around your specific goals — whether that's getting back to running, finishing a renovation, or simply walking the dog without pain.

You'll progress through structured phases of acute management, mobility restoration, strength rebuilding, and functional return. We don't move on until you've passed clear criteria at each stage.

Who This Helps

  • Sports injuries — ankle, knee, hamstring, shoulder
  • Workplace and occupational injuries
  • Falls, accidents and traumatic soft-tissue injuries
  • Chronic overuse and repetitive strain conditions
  • Return-to-activity following long absence

What to Expect

  • Phase-based rehabilitation programme
  • Clear return-to-activity criteria
  • Progressive strength and conditioning
  • Functional movement re-education
  • Discharge plan to prevent re-injury

DBC Certified Neck Rehabilitation

DBC Certified

Chronic neck pain is one of the most poorly treated musculoskeletal conditions worldwide. Most patients cycle through painkillers, generic stretches and short-lived manual therapy with no lasting benefit. The DBC (Dynamic Back Care) cervical protocol exists precisely because the standard approach so often fails.

DBC is a Finnish-developed, internationally recognised rehabilitation system used in over 30 countries. It combines clinical assessment, progressive resistance training, and structured patient education into a programme proven to deliver measurable, lasting improvements in chronic and recurrent neck pain.

I am one of a small number of physiotherapists in the Eastern Cape with the dual DBC Neck & Back certifications. If you've tried other approaches without success, this is the one that's worth your time.

Who This Helps

  • Chronic or recurring neck pain (3+ months)
  • Cervicogenic headaches
  • Postural neck pain from desk-based work
  • Whiplash and post-traumatic cervical pain
  • Patients who haven't responded to other treatments

What to Expect

  • Detailed cervical assessment and outcome measures
  • Structured DBC protocol over 6–12 weeks
  • Supervised progressive resistance training
  • Patient education on long-term self-management
  • Re-assessment with measurable outcomes

DBC Certified Back Rehabilitation in Port Elizabeth

DBC Certified

Lower back pain is the leading cause of disability worldwide. Most cases are mechanical — meaning they come from how the spine is loaded and used, not from structural damage. That's why painkillers and rest rarely solve the problem long-term.

The DBC lumbar protocol is one of the most rigorously researched approaches to chronic and recurrent back pain. It combines clinical assessment, individualised loading exercises, and patient education to restore function and break the cycle of recurrent flare-ups.

For patients in Port Elizabeth and the wider Eastern Cape who have struggled with chronic back pain — sometimes for years — DBC offers a structured, measurable, evidence-based path to lasting recovery.

Who This Helps

  • Chronic or recurrent low back pain
  • Disc-related back pain (with appropriate referral)
  • Post-surgical lumbar rehabilitation
  • Mechanical back pain from work or sport
  • Patients seeking an alternative to surgery

What to Expect

  • Comprehensive lumbar spine assessment
  • Structured 6–12 week DBC programme
  • Progressive loading and strengthening
  • Education on movement and ergonomics
  • Long-term self-management strategy

Post-Operative Physiotherapy

What you do in the weeks and months after orthopaedic surgery often matters more than the surgery itself. A poorly rehabilitated knee replacement, shoulder repair or spinal fusion can leave you with worse function than before the operation. Done properly, post-surgical rehabilitation gets you back to full strength, mobility and confidence.

I work closely with your surgeon's protocols, respecting their timelines while delivering the hands-on treatment, exercise progression and education needed for a smooth recovery. Every programme is individualised to the procedure, your starting point, and your functional goals.

Whether you're recovering from a routine arthroscopy or a major joint replacement, structured physiotherapy is the single biggest predictor of long-term outcome.

Who This Helps

  • Knee surgery — ACL, meniscus, joint replacement
  • Shoulder surgery — rotator cuff, labral repair, replacement
  • Hip replacements and resurfacing
  • Spinal surgery — discectomy, fusion, decompression
  • Ankle, foot and hand orthopaedic procedures

What to Expect

  • Coordination with your surgeon's protocol
  • Phase-based recovery programme
  • Manual therapy and scar management
  • Progressive strengthening and functional re-training
  • Return-to-activity guidance
